### Where Reflet Excels

**Complete Feedback Workflow**: Reflet covers the entire cycle — collect feedback, prioritize with voting, plan on roadmaps, and announce with changelogs. Nolt stops at feedback boards and roadmaps.

**Changelog**: Nolt doesn't offer a changelog feature, so you'd need a separate tool. Reflet includes it natively, keeping your entire feedback loop in one place.

**Open Source & Self-Hosting**: Full code transparency and the ability to host on your own infrastructure for data sovereignty.

**Free Tier**: Reflet offers a generous free plan, while Nolt starts at $29/mo with no free option.

### Where Nolt Excels

**Simplicity**: Nolt is intentionally minimal. If all you need is a clean voting board with zero complexity, Nolt delivers that elegantly.

**Design Polish**: Nolt has a beautifully crafted interface that's pleasant to use, with a focus on aesthetics.

**Single Pricing Tier**: One plan at $29/mo makes the decision simple — no tier comparison needed.

## When to Choose Reflet

- **You want the full feedback loop**: Feedback, roadmaps, and changelogs in one tool
- **Free tier matters**: You want to start without financial commitment
- **Open source is important**: You value code transparency and self-hosting
- **You need AI triage**: Automated feedback categorization saves time
- **GitHub integration is key**: Two-way sync with your development workflow

## When to Choose Nolt

- **Maximum simplicity**: You only need a basic voting board
- **Aesthetics matter most**: Nolt's design is its primary selling point
- **You don't need changelogs**: Your announcement workflow lives elsewhere

## The Verdict

Nolt is a beautiful, simple tool — but it's limited. No changelog, no free tier, and no open-source option.

**Choose Reflet** for a more complete, affordable, and flexible feedback platform.

**Choose Nolt** if you specifically want the most minimal voting board possible and don't mind paying $29/mo for it.
